# 📚 Stanford CS336 (Spring 2024) — My 160+ Pages of Handwritten Class Notes

Welcome! 👋

This repository contains **160+ pages of handwritten notes** that I created while taking **Stanford CS336: Language Modeling from Scratch (Spring 2024)**.

The course, instructed by **Percy Liang** and **Tatsunori Hashimoto**, provides a comprehensive introduction to the principles and systems behind modern Large Language Models (LLMs), covering topics from transformer architectures and pretraining to distributed training, inference, optimization, and evaluation.

---

# Why I Created These Notes

My goal was not simply to pass the course, but to build a deep understanding of how LLMs are developed from the ground up.

Throughout the course, I handwrote detailed notes to reinforce my understanding of the mathematical foundations, implementation details, systems concepts, and engineering trade-offs. Looking back, these notes played an important role in shaping my knowledge of:

- 🧠 Transformer core principles (attention, positional encoding, architecture design)
- 🔤 Tokenization and vocabulary construction (BPE, SentencePiece)
- ⚙️ PyTorch fundamentals and tensor ops (einops) for LLM implementation
- 🧹 Dataset preparation, cleaning, filtering, and corpus engineering
- 🧪 Data-centric techniques (data mixing, rewriting, SFT pipelines)
- 📈 Scaling laws and data–model–compute trade-offs
- 🏗️ Pretraining objectives & training dynamics (causal LM, masked LM)
- 🎯 Fine-tuning strategies (SFT, instruction tuning)
- 🤖 Model alignment (RLHF, DPO, RL algorithms)
- ⚡ Inference systems (latency vs throughput, batching, serving)
- 📏 Evaluation methodologies (perplexity, downstream benchmarks)
- 💻 GPU & TPU architecture + memory optimization
- 📉 Resource accounting (roofline), profiling & performance tuning
- 🌐 Distributed training (data/model/pipeline parallelism)
- 🔗 Collective communication (AllReduce, NCCL)
- 🧩 Mixture of Experts (MoE) and sparse architectures
- 🧬 Kernel-level optimization (CUDA, Triton)
- 🧠 Compiler stacks & graph optimization (XLA)
- 🚀 KV Cache, Flash Attention, efficient attention mechanisms
- 🔢 Mixed precision training (FP16, BF16)
- 🛠️ Checkpointing, fault tolerance, training efficiency
- 📦 System-level optimization (throughput, latency, memory–compute balance)
- 🌍 Deployment & LLM serving systems
- ➕ and more…
